Reconstruction Site
Memory of the original chapel

The chapel is located in Sumvitg, a small alpine village in Graubünden, Switzerland. The region is defined by steep terrain, forests, and seasonal climate extremes. In 1984, an avalanche destroyed the original village chapel, leaving a cultural and spatial void. The new chapel was commissioned as a quiet reconstruction of memory, continuity, and faith.

Cultural Continuity

The surrounding culture is shaped by Roman Catholic traditions and alpine rural life. Architecture in this region is not symbolic, but functional and atmospheric. Wood construction dominates due to material availability and environmental adaptation. The chapel continues this lineage, translating local memory into spatial form.
