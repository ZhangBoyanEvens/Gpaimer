Chapel Project
Small-scale sacred architecture

Peter Zumthor is a Swiss architect known for his focus on atmosphere, material presence, and sensory experience. His work avoids symbolic form, instead emphasizing how spaces are felt through light, texture, and sound. The Chapel of Saint Benedict reflects his early exploration of intimate, site-specific architecture. It translates emotional and physical perception into minimal architectural form.

The chapel is conceived as a spatial envelope rather than a conventional building. Its purpose is to restore a place of worship for the local community after the original chapel was destroyed. The design prioritizes intimacy, silence, and emotional continuity. It avoids monumentality, focusing instead on presence and experience.
